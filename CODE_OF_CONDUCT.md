# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want Subscription Dock to be a welcoming, practical, and respectful project. Participants should:

- Be considerate and assume good intent while still discussing technical evidence directly.
- Give actionable feedback about the work, not the person.
- Respect different experience levels, languages, and backgrounds.
- Accept correction and help repair misunderstandings.

Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, deliberate disruption, doxxing, or publication of private information are not acceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in project spaces and when someone publicly represents the project.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers through the contact method listed in the repository profile. Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants when necessary.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. Maintainers with a conflict of interest should recuse themselves.

## Attribution

This concise policy is informed by the principles of the Contributor Covenant. Projects that need a formal community-governance framework may replace it with the complete current Contributor Covenant text.
